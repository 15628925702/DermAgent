# English Abstract

We present DermAgent, a staged parameterized diagnostic agent for multimodal skin disease classification. DermAgent is built around four core ideas: explicit self-awareness signals, a learnable skill policy, a learned experience bank, and uncertainty-aware aggregation. Instead of relying only on static prompts or fixed rule routing, the system preserves rules as safety priors while introducing a learned controller, final scorer, rule scorer, and evidence calibrator, enabling a practical transition from rule-driven orchestration to parameterized decision making.

We evaluate DermAgent as a staged-validation system on PAD-UFES-20. On a frozen same-model 100-case paired comparison against direct Qwen, DermAgent improves top-1 accuracy from 0.1818 to 0.4300 and malignant recall from 0.7727 to 0.9333, corresponding to absolute gains of +0.2482 and +0.1606, while reducing error rate from 0.0100 to 0.0000. The paired gain is statistically significant (McNemar `p=4.1e-05`), with a bootstrap mean top-1 difference of 0.2504 (95% CI `[0.14, 0.36]`) and a bootstrap mean malignant-recall difference of 0.1777 (95% CI `[0.0732, 0.2941]`). In staged ablation, the clearest additional in-domain gain appears after enabling the learned controller/scorer stack, while retrieval is not yet a stable positive contributor under the current budget. On the full PAD-UFES-20 test-set quality suite (`n=344`), DermAgent achieves top-1 accuracy 0.4012, top-3 accuracy 0.6541, malignant recall 0.9146, Brier score 0.3072, and expected calibration error 0.2588, indicating strong malignancy sensitivity but still weak calibration and uneven class-wise behavior.

These findings show that the learned components have already begun to influence final decisions in measurable ways before full-scale training is complete. More broadly, the paper contributes a practical development pathway for medical AI agents: start from a reliable rule scaffold, then incrementally validate whether controller, memory, calibration, and scoring modules genuinely help.
